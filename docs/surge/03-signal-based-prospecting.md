# Signal-Based Prospecting Playbook
## How Surge Finds Restoration Contractors Ready to Buy

**The principle:** Top performers prospect on signals, not schedules. Responding to a trigger event within 48 hours dramatically increases conversion. This playbook shifts Surge from cold outreach to warm intent-based targeting.

**Time commitment:** 30 min/day to monitor + 2 hrs/week to act on signals. Total: ~4.5 hrs/week.

---

## The Signal Categories (Ranked by Conversion Probability)

### Tier 1 Signals (Respond Within 24 Hours)

**1. Hiring for Operations/Admin Roles**
Restoration companies hiring an "Operations Manager," "Office Manager," "Project Coordinator," or "Billing Specialist" are actively investing in ops. They need tools and systems for that hire to use.

**Where to find:** Indeed, LinkedIn Jobs, ZipRecruiter
**Search terms:** "restoration" + one of: "operations manager", "office manager", "project coordinator", "billing coordinator"
**Outreach angle:** "Saw you're hiring a [role]. The first 90 days of that hire will determine whether they become a force multiplier or just absorb into existing chaos. We help restoration companies set up the ops infrastructure that makes new admin hires productive on day one."

**2. Negative Google Reviews Mentioning Response Time or Communication**
Public pain you can solve. If a homeowner complains about "never heard back," "took weeks to get paid," or "couldn't get an adjuster update," that's a review you can reference directly.

**Where to find:** Google Business Profile search, Yelp, BBB
**How to monitor:** Run a monthly manual audit of target company reviews; or use Reputation.com, Birdeye, or NiceJob for broader tracking
**Outreach angle:** Reference the specific review in the first email. "Saw the review from [first name] last week about the billing delay. We worked with a restoration contractor with the same problem last quarter. Want to show you what we did?"

**3. New Location / Expansion Announcement**
Growth investment = new ops to set up = ideal moment to implement systems rather than retrofit later.

**Where to find:** Local business journals, LinkedIn company page updates, press releases, Chamber of Commerce announcements
**How to monitor:** Google News Alerts for "[target company] expansion" or "[target company] new location"
**Outreach angle:** "Saw the news about the [location] branch. Setting up a second location with clean ops infrastructure is dramatically easier than retrofitting it later. We just did this for a Colorado contractor adding their second branch."

### Tier 2 Signals (Respond Within 48-72 Hours)

**4. Leadership Change / New Hire in Decision Role**
New COO, new operations director, new CFO. Blank slate buyer. No legacy objections.

**Where to find:** LinkedIn (people tab > filter by company > sort by tenure)
**How to monitor:** LinkedIn Sales Navigator alerts for leadership changes at target accounts
**Outreach angle:** "Congratulations on the new role. Most ops leaders I've worked with find their first 90 days easier when there's a data audit on the table. Happy to run one on [company] at no risk if you're open to it."

**5. Competitor Dissatisfaction Signals**
When a restoration company posts reviews complaining about their CRM, field tech software, or billing platform, they're primed for a switch.

**Where to find:** Capterra, G2, Software Advice reviews of JobNimbus, Xactimate, etc.
**How to monitor:** Sign up for Capterra alerts on restoration software reviews
**Outreach angle:** "Saw your review of [software] mentioning [specific pain]. We help restoration companies either fix that with better integrations or migrate to tools that solve it natively. Want to see the options?"

**6. Recent Acquisition or Private Equity Investment**
PE-backed restoration companies have mandate and capital for infrastructure investment.

**Where to find:** PE Hub, Axios Pro Rata, Cleaning & Restoration magazine
**How to monitor:** Google News Alerts for "restoration acquisition" and "restoration private equity"
**Outreach angle:** Lead with data. "Saw [PE firm] acquired [company] last month. Most post-PE restoration businesses lose 6-9 months to infrastructure chaos. We help avoid that."

### Tier 3 Signals (Respond Within 7 Days)

**7. Industry Event Attendance**
IICRC events, Restoration Industry Association (RIA) conferences, local trade shows. Attendance signals investment in improvement.

**Where to find:** Event sponsor lists, RIA member directory, LinkedIn event attendee lists
**How to monitor:** Check RIA and IICRC event pages quarterly

**8. Industry Award or Recognition**
High-receptivity window. They're feeling good. Pride moment.

**Where to find:** Cleaning & Restoration magazine, R&R magazine, RIA awards

**9. Website Redesign / Rebrand**
Investing in customer experience. Align your pitch with their "new chapter."

**Where to find:** Wayback Machine comparisons quarterly; LinkedIn company page updates

## Monitoring Tool Stack (Set Up Once, Runs Forever)

### Free / Low-Cost Tools

**Google Alerts** (free)
Set up alerts for:
- "restoration contractor expansion"
- "restoration company hiring"
- "[specific target company name]" (set one per target)
- "restoration private equity"
- "Xactimate" + "integration"
- "Encircle" + "[city name]" (e.g., "Encircle Phoenix")

Delivery: "As-it-happens" or "daily digest" to your prospecting email inbox.

**Google News Alerts**
Same as above but news-specific. Often catches press releases faster than regular Google Alerts.

**LinkedIn Sales Navigator** ($99/mo, best ROI)
Build saved searches for:
- "Operations Manager" at restoration companies in target markets
- "Owner" at restoration companies changing jobs
- "COO" at restoration companies with 10-50 employees

Set alerts for job changes, new posts, and new connections of target personas.

**Indeed Job Alerts** (free)
Keyword alerts for:
- "restoration" + "operations manager"
- "restoration" + "billing coordinator"
- "water damage" + "project coordinator"
- Filter by radius (target metros)

**Capterra Alerts** (free)
Alert for new reviews on:
- JobNimbus
- Xactimate
- Encircle
- Symbility
- Albi

Watch for reviews mentioning pain points Surge solves.

### Paid Tools (When Volume Justifies)

**Crunchbase Pro** ($49/mo)
Track PE investments in restoration sector. Filter by industry, deal size, geography.

**Apollo.io** ($59-$99/mo)
Build lead lists of target accounts with contact info. Integrates with outreach sequences.

**Clay.com** ($149/mo)
Advanced enrichment. Connects multiple signals into a single prospect record.

## Target Account Tiering

Build a target list of 150 restoration contractors in the US. Tier them using the ICP scoring framework.

### ICP Scoring Dimensions (Score 1-5 Each)

| Dimension | Score 1 | Score 3 | Score 5 |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| Business type fit | Only commercial, different trade | Mixed commercial/residential | Full-service restoration (water/fire/mold), residential-heavy |
| Annual revenue | Under $500K or over $20M | $500K-$1M or $10M-$20M | $1M-$10M |
| Current CRM | Enterprise (DASH, ServiceTitan) | Restoration-specific but small (iRestore, JobDox) | JobNimbus or nothing formal |
| Online presence | Dead website, few reviews | Decent website, 4.0-4.4 stars | Modern site, 4.5+ stars, active social |
| Owner accessibility | Enterprise/PE-owned | Partnership or family | Solo owner with public LinkedIn |
| Geography | Remote state, low disaster rate | Mixed | Disaster-prone states (FL, TX, CO, CA, OK) with high claim volume |

**Scoring:**
- 24-30: Tier 1 (top 15-20% of target list, 20-30 accounts)
- 18-23: Tier 2 (next 30%, 45-60 accounts)
- Below 18: Tier 3 (remaining, automated nurture only)

### Dynamic Tier Promotion

Any Tier 3 account that triggers a Tier 1 signal (hiring, negative review, expansion, leadership change) gets promoted to Tier 1 immediately.

## Tier 1 Outreach Protocol

Full personalized multi-channel. 8-12 touches over 10 days.

### The 5-Minute Research Sprint (Before Every Tier 1 Outreach)

1. **LinkedIn (90 sec):** Owner's posts, tenure, growth content
2. **Google (60 sec):** "[Company name] news" - press, awards, expansion
3. **Google Reviews (60 sec):** Customer complaints, competitor mentions
4. **Their website (60 sec):** What they emphasize, CX messaging, any claims specialization
5. **Indeed/LinkedIn Jobs (30 sec):** Who are they hiring?

**Output:** One specific, real observation to lead with. This is the difference between 3% reply rate and 15%.

## Daily Prospecting Routine (30 min/day)

### Morning Block (15 min)
- Check Google Alerts digest for new signals
- Check LinkedIn Sales Nav notifications for target account activity
- Check Indeed alerts for new relevant job postings
- Flag any Tier 1 signals for same-day action

### Afternoon Block (15 min)
- Monthly Google Reviews check on 5-10 target accounts (rotate through the list)
- Scan industry Facebook groups for pain points (RIA Members group, various restoration-specific groups)
- Add any new signals to your tracking spreadsheet

### End of Week (45 min)
- Review signals collected
- Execute research sprints on 3-5 Tier 1 accounts
- Send initial outreach on Tier 1 targets
- Update tracking sheet: new tiers, promoted accounts, responses

## Signal Tracking Spreadsheet Template

Use Google Sheets or a simple Notion database. Columns:

| Company | Contact | Tier | Signal Type | Signal Date | First Outreach | Last Touch | Stage | Notes |

**Stages:**
- Unreached
- First touch sent
- Multi-touch in progress
- Replied - booked call
- Replied - not interested
- Audit booked
- Audit complete
- Retainer signed
- Dormant (no response after 10 touches)

**Review cadence:** Weekly. Anything in "Multi-touch in progress" for more than 14 days gets flagged for pattern interrupt or breakup touch.

## Measurement & Optimization

### Weekly Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| New signals captured | 10+ |
| New Tier 1 outreach initiated | 3-5 |
| Meetings booked | 1-2 |
| Audits booked | 0-1 |

### Monthly Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Signal-to-meeting conversion | 15-25% |
| Meeting-to-audit conversion | 40-60% |
| Audit-to-retainer conversion | 40-60% |
| Net new retainer MRR | $5K-$15K |

### Quarterly Retro Questions

1. Which signals produced the best meetings? Double down.
2. Which signals wasted time? Remove from monitoring.
3. Which outreach hooks got the highest reply rates? Template them.
4. Which markets are converting fastest? Concentrate there.

## Why Most Agencies Get This Wrong

Most home service marketing agencies use spray-and-pray prospecting: buy a list, blast cold emails, hope for 1-2% reply rates. They mistake activity for progress.

Signal-based prospecting trades volume for precision. You make 10 moves per week instead of 1,000. But each move is anchored to a real buying signal, which is why reply rates hit 15-25% instead of 1-2%, and why the meetings that come out of it are with buyers who are actually ready to move.

This is the unfair advantage that lets Surge punch above its weight class while Austin only has 10 hrs/week to invest.
