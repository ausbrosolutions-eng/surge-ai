# Jared Watts Champion Activation Playbook

**Context:** Jared is Branch Manager at Rehab Restoration and the strongest internal champion Surge currently has. He's invested in the outcome, understands the value, and has a network of peer restoration professionals. Activating him as a distribution channel could unlock 3-10 warm introductions within 30 days.

**Goal:** Convert Jared from "stakeholder at a client engagement" to "active referral source with aligned incentives."

---

## The Champion Test (Diagnostic)

Before making the ask, confirm Jared is a real champion vs. a friendly coach:

**Test 1:** Next time you're on a call, ask: "Would you be willing to set up a 15-minute call between me and someone in your network who might benefit from what we're building here?"

- If he says "Yeah, let me think about who" and follows up within 7 days = **Champion**
- If he says "Sure" and nothing happens = **Coach** (friendly but not selling internally)

**Test 2:** Ask him directly: "If the results we're projecting for Rehab actually land, would you feel comfortable telling other restoration owners about what we did?"

- If he gets animated and starts naming people = **Champion**
- If he's non-committal = **Coach**

You can skip both tests if your gut already says champion. Move to activation.

---

## The Activation Conversation

**When to have it:**
After Phase 1 delivers a visible win. Specifically, after the Collection SMS sequence has been live for 30 days and recovered its first $20K+ in previously stuck invoices. Jared seeing money come in from a system he advocated for = peak champion moment.

**Where:** In person if possible. Over a beer, lunch, or a job site visit. Not Zoom. This is a relationship conversation, not a business meeting.

**Duration:** 20-30 minutes.

### The Script

**Opening (builds context):**
"Jared, I want to talk to you about something a little different than our usual agenda. You've been instrumental in what's happening at Rehab. The reason our audit actually hit home wasn't just the data. It was because you were willing to tell me the truth about what was broken. The RIB uploads, the adjuster stonewalling, the documentation gap. That honesty is what made the whole roadmap work."

**The ask (direct, no beating around the bush):**
"Here's what I want to propose. I'd like to turn you into a real distribution channel for Surge. You know restoration owners. You go to industry events. You've probably got a dozen peers who are dealing with a version of what Rehab was dealing with before we showed up. I want to make it worth your time to introduce us."

**The offer (three options, let him pick):**

"There are three ways we can structure this. Tell me which one feels right to you:

**Option 1 - Referral fee:** 10% of the first-year retainer revenue from any client you introduce who signs. That's $600-$1,200 per month for the first 12 months on a typical engagement. If you refer 3 clients that convert, you're looking at $25K-$40K in the first year.

**Option 2 - Credit toward Rehab:** Every conversion knocks $3,000 off Rehab's retainer for the following 3 months. You control the savings, Scott and Aaron see the value, everybody wins.

**Option 3 - Revenue share hybrid:** 5% referral fee + $2,000 credit to Rehab per conversion. Smaller immediate cash but you get both benefits.

Whatever structure you pick, I'll put it in writing as a simple referral agreement so there's no ambiguity. Your reputation is on the line when you make an intro. I want to make sure it's worth the risk."

**The commitment ask:**
"If you're open to this, I'd love to get three warm introductions in the next 30 days. Not a list of 20 people. Three specific owners in your network who you think would actually benefit. I'll take it from there. You don't need to sell for me. You just need to say 'This guy ran an audit on us that was legit, you should hear him out.'"

**Close:**
"Think about it. No rush. Text me this week with which option feels right."

## Jared's Target List (Build This Together)

Ask Jared to help build a target list. Offer him a framework to brainstorm from:

**Direct peers:**
- Other restoration owners he's met at IICRC training, trade shows, or local associations
- Rehab's former or current subcontractor partners
- Restoration companies in adjacent markets (Denver, Colorado Springs, Pueblo) that Rehab has worked alongside

**Industry connections:**
- Adjusters or public adjusters who work with multiple restoration companies
- TPAs or MRP administrators (Contractor Connection, Westhill, Accuserve contacts)
- Xactimate trainers or Verisk reps he knows
- Insurance agents who refer restoration work

**Adjacent trades:**
- Roofing contractors who handle insurance claims work (same workflow pain)
- Flood mitigation specialists (usually smaller, similar systems gap)
- Mold remediation specialists

**Online network:**
- Restoration industry Facebook groups he's active in (identify 3-5)
- LinkedIn connections with "Owner" or "Operations" in their title at restoration companies

Ask him to star 5-10 names that come to mind first. Those are the warmest intros.

## Champion Development (Ongoing)

Treat Jared as a long-term asset, not a one-time referral source.

### Monthly rituals

**Ship him wins:**
Once a month, send Jared a one-paragraph update about what's working across Surge clients. Not Rehab-specific. Industry-wide patterns. This gives him ammunition to mention at events and on calls.

Example: "Quick update - we just finished an audit for a Phoenix-based restoration contractor and found $340K in stuck collections on a $2.8M annual revenue business. Same pattern as Rehab. Pretty sure every restoration shop has a version of this problem. Thought you'd appreciate the data point."

**Ask for feedback:**
Every 60 days, ask Jared: "What would make the Rehab engagement better? What's still frustrating? Where should we be doing more?" His honesty is a gift. Show him you listen.

**Make him look smart:**
When Jared makes a referral that converts, share the outcome publicly (with permission). A LinkedIn post: "Grateful to Jared Watts for the introduction that led to us working with [company]. These kinds of relationships are how good work spreads." Makes Jared proud. Signals to his network he's connected to quality work.

### Quarterly

**Send him something:**
Box of steaks to his house. Branded Surge apparel. Tickets to a Rockies game. Not "business expense" mindset. Relationship investment mindset.

**Check in off-agenda:**
Call him with zero agenda once a quarter. "Hey man, just wanted to check in. How's the family, how's the team, anything you want to vent about?" He'll remember the calls where you didn't want something from him.

## Multi-Threading Beyond Jared

Jared is your strongest champion at Rehab but he shouldn't be your only one. Build parallel relationships:

- **Scott (owner):** Monthly leadership check-in. Strategic conversations. Positions Surge as Scott's peer, not just a vendor.
- **Zoie (production tracking):** She's being trained on Claude on April 23. Her success story is your case study. Interview her 60 days in about how her workflow changed.
- **Silvia (intake):** Same as Zoie. Her liberation from manual WhatsApp > JN entry is a 2-minute testimonial video waiting to be filmed.

Three internal champions at Rehab means if any one of them leaves or changes roles, the relationship survives. 40% of deals stall due to champion churn. Don't be in that 40%.

---

## Red Flags That Jared Is Not Your Champion

Watch for these signs that the relationship isn't what you thought:

- Jared agrees to refer but never does (more than 30 days with zero movement)
- He has a reason every time: "Hey, I haven't gotten around to it yet, been slammed"
- He doesn't introduce you verbally at events you both attend
- He doesn't share positive LinkedIn content you post about Rehab's progress
- When you ask for feedback, he's consistently vague or positive-but-empty

If multiple red flags show up, reassess. He might be a great operator, a friendly voice at Rehab, and a good relationship, but not a champion. Don't push. Focus on multi-threading to other Rehab stakeholders and building champions at your next 2-3 clients.
