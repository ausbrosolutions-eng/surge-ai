# Email Cadence Templates
## 5-Touch Sequence for Restoration Contractor Outreach

**Who this is for:** Owners, COOs, Operations Directors at restoration contractors doing $1M-$15M annually, with 5-50 employees.

**Cadence structure:** 5 emails over 21 days, paired with LinkedIn touches and a phone call.

**Rules:**
- Never start an email with "I" or "My name is"
- Subject lines under 50 characters
- Body under 120 words
- One specific, real observation per email
- Soft CTA on every touch (not "book a demo")

## LinkedIn Touches (Paired With Email Cadence)

### Day 1 - View profile (no message)
Simply view their LinkedIn profile the same day as Touch 1. This creates a "viewed your profile" notification, making your name familiar when the email arrives.

### Day 4 - Connection request
Send a connection request with a brief, non-pitchy note:

"Hey [First Name], been researching restoration contractors in [market] as part of a project on ops infrastructure trends. Would love to connect. No pitch, just building my network in the space."

### Day 6 - Phone call
Call the business directly. Be warm with whoever answers:

"Hey, is [First Name] available by any chance? Tell them Austin from Surge Advisory, following up on an email conversation about operations infrastructure."

If gatekeepered: "No worries. Could I leave my name and number? Just let [him/her] know I reached out."

### Day 14 - LinkedIn DM (if connected)
If they accepted the connection request:

"Hey [First Name], following up on email. Wanted to make sure it didn't end up in spam. No pitch, just wanted to share one quick data point that comes up in almost every restoration audit we run. Let me know if you're open to it."

## Video Prospecting (Elite Tier 1 Move)

For highest-priority Tier 1 accounts only. Massively increases reply rates because almost nobody in home service marketing does this.

### Setup (one time)
- Install Loom (free)
- Set up a branded video thumbnail template
- Practice the 60-second pitch

### The Video Script (45-60 seconds)

"Hey [First Name], Austin Brooks at Surge Advisory. Not going to pitch you anything, I just wanted to show you a quick screen share.

[Share screen showing their Google Reviews or Google Business Profile]

So you can see right here on your Google profile, [specific observation - a review, a rating trend, a missing element]. I notice this same pattern on almost every restoration contractor we audit. It typically means there's a collections or documentation gap on the back end causing customer experience issues on the front end.

We ran a 2-week audit on a Colorado contractor about your size and found $1.3M stuck in their 5.0 queue. Built the automation to recover it. I'd bet your business has a version of the same problem.

Worth 15 minutes to see what we'd find? I'll put the link in the email. Talk soon."

### How to send
- Loom generates a link + thumbnail
- Paste into the email body with a play button overlay
- Subject: "Quick screen share for [Company Name]"
- Body: "Hey [First Name], made a 60-second screen share for you. Link below."

**Reply rate on video prospecting: 25-40%. Normal email reply rate: 2-4%.**

## Email Performance Benchmarks

Track these metrics weekly:

| Metric | Minimum | Target | Elite |
|--------|---------|--------|-------|
| Open rate (Touch 1) | 35% | 50% | 65%+ |
| Reply rate (Touch 1) | 3% | 8% | 15%+ |
| Reply rate (full sequence) | 8% | 15% | 25%+ |
| Meeting-to-audit conversion | 30% | 50% | 70%+ |

**If open rate is below 35%:** Problem is subject line or sender reputation. Check deliverability (GlockApps, mail-tester.com).

**If reply rate is below 3% with good opens:** Problem is the body. Make opening line more specific. Reduce pitch-to-personalization ratio.

**If replies don't convert to meetings:** Problem is the CTA. Soft asks convert better than "book a demo."

## Sending Infrastructure

### Tools
- **Email sending:** Instantly ($37/mo) or Apollo ($59/mo) for sequence automation
- **Email warmup:** Instantly has built-in; alternatively Warmup Inbox ($15/mo)
- **Video:** Loom (free for 25 videos/month; $8/mo unlimited)
- **Email verification:** NeverBounce or ZeroBounce to clean lists before sending

### Domain setup for deliverability
- **Primary domain:** Use a secondary domain for cold outreach (e.g., getsurgeadvisory.co) so you don't burn surgeadvisory.co reputation
- **SPF, DKIM, DMARC:** Must be set up properly on sending domain
- **Warmup period:** 2-4 weeks of gradual volume ramp before cold outreach at scale
- **Daily send limit:** Max 30-50 cold emails/day per inbox during first 60 days

### Inbox rotation
If scaling volume, run 2-3 inboxes on the same domain (austin@, hello@, team@) with sequence tool routing sends between them.

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

❌ **"Hope this finds you well"** - Dead opening. Signals templated spam.
❌ **"Quick question"** - Overused. Everyone uses it. Stands out for being unoriginal.
❌ **"I came across your business and thought..."** - Starts with "I" and sounds like a bot.
❌ **Feature lists** - "We offer X, Y, Z..." Nobody cares about your feature list.
❌ **"Jump on a quick call"** - The word "quick" never is. Be specific: "15 minutes."
❌ **Generic subject lines** - "Partnership opportunity" = instant delete.
❌ **Long first emails** - Anything over 125 words on Touch 1 kills reply rate.
❌ **Multiple CTAs** - One ask per email. Always.
❌ **Sending on Fridays or Mondays before 9am** - Deliverability and response both drop.

## Segmentation Variants

### For Adjacent Trades (Testing Phase Only)

**Roofing contractors with insurance work:**
Same pain patterns (supplement cycle, documentation gap, collection delays) but different terminology. Replace "restoration" with "roofing" and "mitigation" with "claim work" throughout.

**Plumbing with water mitigation divisions:**
Frame as "the water damage side of your business." Emphasize supplement and adjuster workflow pain.

**Mold remediation specialists:**
Smaller companies, similar pain. Often use JobNimbus or nothing. Focus on documentation compliance and insurance workflow.

**Do not start with these segments.** Win restoration first, then expand. Focus beats breadth in year one.
