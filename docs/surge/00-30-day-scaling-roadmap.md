# The Surge 30-Day Scaling Roadmap
## From "Austin With A Spreadsheet" to "Real Business With A Flywheel"

**Context:** Austin has 10 hrs/week for Surge outside of Cherry. One paying client (Rehab Restoration). First revenue landed ($970.10 in OpEx). The Cherry playbook has been installed. The strategy docs are built. Now it's execution time.

**The goal:** In 30 days, Surge goes from a consulting practice running on hustle to a consulting practice running on infrastructure. Revenue target: $15K-$25K new MRR pipeline by Day 30.

**Total time commitment:** 4-6 hrs weekend for setup, 8-10 hrs/week ongoing for execution.

## Weekly Operating Rhythm (Ongoing, After Day 30)

### Daily (30-45 min)
- Morning: signal check, respond to any Tier 1 signal within 2 hours
- Afternoon: inbox + LinkedIn + HubSpot update
- Evening (M/W/F): 1 LinkedIn post or comment on industry discussion

### Tuesday & Thursday (2 hrs)
- Send/schedule outreach sequences
- Execute discovery calls (these are the highest-value 45-min blocks of the week)
- Follow-up emails within 2 hours of every call

- Watch 1 recorded call, take notes
- Update HubSpot stages
- Plan next week's outreach

### Month End (3 hrs)
- Full metrics review
- Channel performance analysis

- Knowledge base refresh

## The Existential Question

At Day 90, the numbers will tell you the answer to a bigger question: is Surge a real business or a hobby?

If MRR is at $13K+ with healthy pipeline momentum, the answer is "real business, plan the Cherry transition thoughtfully over the next 6 months."

If MRR is below $8K with weak pipeline, the answer is either "the positioning is wrong" or "the targeting is wrong" or "the offer is wrong." Don't push harder on broken mechanics. Diagnose and adjust.

**The only failure mode that matters:** running this for 6-12 months without measurement and never knowing what worked or didn't. Everything in this 30-day roadmap is designed to prevent that failure mode.

---

## Final Principle

Austin has 10 hours a week. That is simultaneously a severe constraint and the greatest feature of this plan. Every hour has to be leveraged through infrastructure. Hustle doesn't scale. Systems do.

The entire flywheel - signal monitoring, scored pipeline, automated cadences, recorded calls, productized audit, referral engine - is designed to do the work FOR Austin, freeing his 10 hours for the only two things that cannot be automated: recording case studies and closing deals.

That is the only version of Surge that ever becomes more than a side project.
