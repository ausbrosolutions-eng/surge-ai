# Sales, Enablement & RevOps Intelligence
## Universal Principles for Surge Advisory Client Engagements

Source: Cherry (fintech) enablement playbook, adapted for Surge Advisory's agency work.
Applies to: Our own agency sales, AND the sales/ops/enablement systems we build for clients.

When a Surge client asks us to improve their sales process, build their outreach system, set up their CRM, or train their team, apply these frameworks to their specific business context. Always ask clarifying questions to ground advice in their actual operation before recommending.

## 1. Business Economics Foundation

Before writing a single email, building a cadence, or recommending a tool, understand the three numbers that drive every B2B service business:

- **Revenue Generated:** Total dollar value of work sold/delivered
- **Collection Rate:** What the business actually receives vs. what it invoices. Healthy = 95%+. Below 85% is a problem worth diagnosing. (Rehab Restoration is at ~70% effective collection rate due to the $1.3M stuck at 5.0.)
- **Overhead/Cost of Delivery:** Running costs as % of revenue. Lean = 40-55%. Average = 55-70%. Struggling = 80%+.

**Your product either:** increases revenue, improves collection rate, reduces overhead, or reduces risk. Know which before pitching.

**The lost revenue calculation is the most powerful closer.** If a prospect is losing X customers/month to a solvable problem, and average deal is $Y, the cost of inaction is X * Y * 12 per year. Always help the prospect calculate this themselves. Never just state it.

## 2. Stakeholder Mapping (Decision Authority by Size)

**Small business / owner-operator (1-10 employees):**
Owner IS the buyer. Speak in business outcomes. Avoid jargon. Be warm with gatekeepers (spouse, office manager).

**Small-to-mid (10-50 employees):**
Owner + Ops Manager. The manager is often the most important influencer - get them genuinely excited and they'll sell internally for you.

**Specialist/Department-led (any size):**
There's often a "treatment coordinator" equivalent -- a person whose whole job is handling proposals/objections. This is your champion. Train them.

**Mid-market (50-200):**
Ops Director, VP. Analytical buyers. Respond to data, ROI, risk language. Multi-threading mandatory.

**Enterprise / Multi-location:**
Corporate/regional level (COO, VP Ops). 8-16 week cycle. Pitch: standardization, consolidated reporting, volume pricing.

**Universal truth:** Associate-level roles have zero purchasing authority. Be warm but don't burn selling time there.

## 3. Buyer Psychology

**Incumbents persist through inertia, not merit.** Switching feels like work. Your job is making the cost of staying feel higher than the cost of switching.

**Three customer types:**
1. **Doesn't need help** (happy, solved) -- move on
2. **Right on the edge** -- could manage without you, but you make things better. **This is where most unexpected lift comes from.** Most sellers miss this segment.
3. **Clearly needs help** (obvious pain)

**Most sellers only target type 3. Fastest-growing businesses pitch to ALL qualified prospects as a standard option -- type 2 is where the volume lives.**

**What buyers care about, in order:**
1. Customer experience (friction = won't adopt)
2. Staff burden (extra steps = resistance)
3. Cash flow / speed of ROI
4. Risk reduction (who carries risk if it fails)
5. Revenue growth (comes AFTER trust is established)
6. Reputation (solutions that could embarrass them get disqualified)

## 4. SPIN Selling (The Universal Questioning Framework)

Origin: Neil Rackham, 35,000+ analyzed sales calls. Core insight: in complex sales, best reps question better, not pitch harder. Buyers who articulate their own pain are far more likely to move forward.

### The Four Question Types

**S - Situation:** Establish facts. Used early. Keep minimal (3-4 max). Do research beforehand.
- "What do you currently use for [X]?"
- "How many [customers/jobs] per month?"
- "Average [deal/job] value?"

**P - Problem:** Surface frustrations. Most discovery lives here. Get them to say the problem out loud.
- "When [current solution] fails, what happens?"
- "How often does [cost/quality/capacity] come up as a reason customers don't move forward?"
- "Has your team ever lost a customer because of [this gap]?"

**I - Implication:** This is where SPIN separates from standard discovery. Take the problem and explore downstream consequences. The prospect calculates the damage themselves.
- "If that customer doesn't move forward, do they come back, or is that revenue lost?"
- "At your current rate, how many [customers] per month are you losing to this?"
- "If each is worth $X, what does that add up to over a year?"
- "Has this ever come back on the business -- a complaint or review you didn't see coming?"

**N - Need-Payoff:** Ask the prospect to describe the value of solving it in their own words.
- "If you could solve [X], what would that mean for your [revenue/team]?"
- "If [painful task] took 2 minutes instead of 20, how would that change things?"

### Implied vs. Explicit Needs

**Implied:** "Yeah, we lose some customers who can't afford it." (Felt pain, not actionable)
**Explicit:** "We're losing $15K/month in declined work and we need to solve this." (Buying signal)

**SPIN's job:** Move prospects from implied to explicit.

### SPIN Mistakes to Avoid
- Too many Situation questions (feels like a survey)
- Skipping to pitch before pain is surfaced
- Weak Implication questions ("so that's a problem?")
- Filling silence after Need-Payoff questions -- let them finish
- Treating every call as one-call close -- SPIN is multi-touch

## 5. PACI Discovery Framework

SPIN is the questioning methodology. PACI is the content map. They layer together.

| PACI | SPIN | Purpose |
|------|------|---------|
| **P**ain | Problem | What's broken today? |
| **A**wareness | Situation | What have they tried? What do they know? |
| **C**onsequence | Implication | What does inaction cost? |
| **I**mpact | Need-Payoff | What would solving it be worth? |

## 6. Email Writing Rules

- Under 120 words total
- Never start with "I" or "My name is"
- Subject lines: specific, curiosity-driven, under 50 chars, no spam triggers (ASAP, Last Chance, Free)
- Opening line: reference one specific, real thing about their business
- Body: 2-3 sentences max. One concrete benefit tied to pain. One proof point.
- CTA: soft -- "Worth a 15-minute call?" Never "schedule a demo" as first ask

**Better phrasing:**
| Instead of... | Say... |
|---------------|--------|
| "We help X businesses with Y" | "We work with [X] to close [specific gap] -- the customers who want to say yes but can't [reason]" |
| "We have high approval rate" | "[Competitor] delivers X%. For a business seeing 100 customers/month, that's [math]. We deliver Y%" |
| "It's easy to use" | "The [process] takes under [time] on a [phone/tablet]. Most teams have it running by the time the customer walks out" |
| "You'll get paid fast" | "You receive [outcome] within [timeframe] of [trigger], regardless of what happens after" |
| "We're cheaper" | "At standard pricing, we're X% below [incumbent]. At [volume tier], Y% below what you're paying per transaction" |

## 7. Five-Touch Email Cadence

- **Touch 1 (Day 1) - Cold Open:** Signal-anchored observation + specific lift hook. <100 words. Soft CTA.
- **Touch 2 (Day 4) - Social Proof:** "A [similar business] switched from [incumbent], [result]." One sentence of proof.
- **Touch 3 (Day 8) - Pain Agitation:** "[X]% of [lost/declined] customers never come back. Here's how we close that gap."
- **Touch 4 (Day 14) - Pattern Interrupt:** "No reply usually means: 1) Not a priority. 2) Already solved. 3) My emails got buried. Which one?"
- **Touch 5 (Day 21) - Breakup:** Soft close. Leave door open. Share a lasting insight.

## 8. Multi-Channel 10-Day Sprint

Omnichannel outperforms single-channel significantly. Personalized emails get 2x reply rates.

- Day 1 (Email): Signal-anchored cold, <125 words
- Day 1 (LinkedIn): View profile, no message (creates familiarity notification)
- Day 3 (Email): Follow-up with specific stat/case study
- Day 4 (LinkedIn): Connection request with brief, non-pitchy note
- Day 6 (Phone): Call, ask for decision-maker by name, reference the email
- Day 8 (Email): Share relevant insight (lost revenue math, industry stat)
- Day 10 (Final): Pattern interrupt or soft breakup

**Video prospecting (massively underused):**
45-60 second Loom/video in outreach can significantly increase reply rates.
- Open with observation about their specific business in first 5 seconds
- Personalize thumbnail (show their business name/website on your screen)
- Almost no reps in any industry do this -- real competitive advantage

## 9. Account Tiering

**Core mistake:** treating all accounts equally. When everything is Tier 1, nothing is.

**Tier 1 (top 15-20%):** Highest ICP fit + active buying signal. Fully personalized multi-channel.
**Tier 2 (next 30%):** Good ICP fit, no active signal. Semi-personalized sequences. Promote when trigger fires.
**Tier 3 (remaining):** ICP-adjacent. Automated sequences only. Watch for signals.

**ICP Scoring Dimensions (1-5 each):**
1. Business type fit
2. Current solution (weak incumbent = 5, strong/satisfied = 1)
3. Deal/job value signal
4. Online presence quality (reviews, modern site, social)
5. Decision-maker accessibility
6. Geography/market fit

**Dynamic Tiering:** A Tier 3 account that posts a job listing related to your product, gets a negative review about a gap you solve, or announces expansion -- immediately becomes Tier 1.

## 10. Trigger Events (Prospect on Signal, Not Schedule)

Speed matters: responding within 48 hours of a trigger event dramatically increases conversion.

| Signal | Meaning | Angle |
|--------|---------|-------|
| New location opening | Growth investment | "Setting up [X] from day one is easier than retrofitting" |
| Hiring in relevant role | Investing in that function | They need tools to arm that hire |
| Negative review about gap you solve | Visible public pain | Direct displacement |
| Competitor negative review | Unhappy with incumbent | Direct displacement |
| Recent rebrand / new website | Investing in experience | "Align with your new chapter" |
| Industry event attendance | Actively investing | Post-event: "Saw you were at [Conference]..." |
| Industry award/recognition | Pride moment, receptive | High-receptivity outreach |
| Leadership change / new hire in decision role | Blank slate | First impression, no legacy objections |

**How to find signals:**
- Google Alerts for business/owner name
- LinkedIn alerts (job changes, posts, new hires)
- Job boards (Indeed/LinkedIn) -- hiring = investment
- Google Reviews monthly for competitor/pain mentions
- Local news / press releases

## 11. Five-Minute Research Sprint (Before Every Tier 1)

1. **LinkedIn (90s):** Recent posts? Tenure? Growth/challenge content?
2. **Google (60s):** "[Business] news" -- press, awards, new locations?
3. **Google Reviews (60s):** Customer complaints? Mentions of gap you solve?
4. **Website (60s):** What do they emphasize? CX messaging?
5. **Job Postings (30s):** Hiring in relevant role?

**Goal:** Find one specific, real thing. Lead with it. Separates 3% reply rate from 15%.

## 12. Discovery Call Execution

**Pre-call (5 min prep):** Know business type, current solution, pain signals. Identify likely stakeholders. Send 2-line agenda morning-of.

**During:** 70% listening, 30% talking.
- Within 10 min: "Who else typically weighs in on decisions like this?" (Plant multi-threading seed early)
- Ask buying process: "If your team decided to move forward, what does getting a new vendor set up typically look like?"
- Reserve last 5 min: confirm specific next step with specific date

**Close call by confirming math together:**
"So if you're [current state] at [metric], you're losing roughly [X] per month. At [value] average, that's [$amount] per month. If [solution] gets you to [improved metric], you're recovering most of that. Does that feel about right?"

**Goal is Advance, not close:**
- **Advance:** Specific next step with date
- **Continuation:** "Let me think about it, call me next week" -- NOT a real next step

## 13. Competitive Positioning

**Positioning rule:** Position as the primary solution, not an add-on. Never "run alongside [incumbent]" -- pitch as the replacement.

**Incumbent displacement script:**
1. Acknowledge the relationship
2. Reframe history ("Most set that up 5-10 years ago and never re-evaluated")
3. Surface cost of inaction
4. Make the switch feel easy

**Be honest about incumbent strengths.** Name 2-3 things they genuinely do well. Then name 3-4 things you win on. Builds trust.

**"We already have [Competitor]":**
"Makes sense. Most businesses in your space use them. Question is whether it's performing at the level it was when you signed up. [Specific stat where you outperform]. Worth 15 minutes to see the math?"

## 14. Objection Handling Patterns

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We already have [Competitor]" | Acknowledge > reframe as outdated evaluation > surface gap > make switch easy |
| "We're locked in a contract" | "No exclusivity required. What's your renewal timeline?" |
| "Our customers don't ask for this" | "Most who need [X] don't ask -- they just leave or say no. [Stat]" |
| "We tried similar and it didn't work" | "What specifically made it not work?" > diagnose > show how you address |
| "I need to talk to [DM]" | "Makes sense. Want me to put together a one-pager, or set up a quick 15-min call with both?" |
| "We don't have time" | "[Solution] requires almost nothing from your team -- [specific easy thing]" |
| "Too expensive" | Never defend on price. Surface cost of inaction first. "What's [problem] costing you today per month?" |
| "Need to think about it" | "Fair. What's the main thing you'd want clear before making a call?" |

## 15. Champion Development

A champion closes deals when you're not in the room. Most reps identify one. Top reps invest.

**Test if you have a champion vs. coach:**
Ask them to set up a 15-minute call with the decision maker.
- They do it = **champion**
- They stall = **coach** (friendly but not selling for you)

**How to equip a champion:**
- Give them the math already done for their business size/type
- Teach them how to answer the DM's likely objections
- Ask directly: "What's the biggest obstacle to getting this approved?" Then clear it
- Make them look smart internally. Give them a win to present.
- Check in proactively

**Multi-threading:**
- 40% of deals stall because primary contact left or lost support
- Always identify 3+ stakeholders: Economic Buyer, Champion, End User
- After every meeting, follow-up email includes ALL stakeholders

## 16. Pipeline Discipline

**Most important question for every open deal:**
"What is the specific next step and when is it scheduled?"
**Any deal without a specific next step with a date is not a real deal.**

**Daily (90-min morning block):**
- 20 min: Review pipeline. Each deal: what's the next step and when?
- 40 min: Prospecting -- outreach, follow-ups, LinkedIn, calls
- 30 min: Prep for today's calls

**Weekly review:**
- Every deal has scheduled next step with date?
- Which haven't moved in 14+ days? What's the play?
- Multi-threaded at every Tier 1?
- What triggers fired this week on Tier 2/3?
- What new Tier 1 accounts to add this week?

**Pipeline math:** Work backward from quota. If close rate = 25%, need 4x quota in qualified pipeline (3x minimum). Below 3x = stop everything and prospect.

**Urgency rule:** Deals closing within 50 days have dramatically higher win rates than deals past 50 days. Momentum is everything.

## 17. Top Performer Mental Models

1. Own the pipeline. Prospect daily regardless of health.
2. Every deal needs specific next step with specific date. No exceptions.
3. Speed wins. Respond to triggers within hours.

6. Treat gatekeeper like decision maker.
7. 80% of sales need 5+ follow-ups. Most stop at 2-3.
8. Multi-threading is competitive advantage because 78% of reps are single-threaded.

## 18. Revenue Enablement Stack (The Flywheel)

```
Reality Capture (calls, emails, deals)
    v
Intelligence Layer (scoring, analysis, signal detection)
    v
Practice Layer (roleplay, scenarios, skill building)
    v
Knowledge Layer (playbooks, battle cards, templates)
    v
Execution Layer (sequences, cadences in market)
    v
Back to Reality Capture (calls improve, repeat)
```

**Most common failure:** Tools intake data but output pipes are never built. Engine runs, nobody drives.

### Tool Category 1: Conversation Intelligence (Gong, Chorus)
**Should:** Record every call, score vs methodology, surface coaching opportunities, feed insights to rest of stack.
**Usually does:** Records calls. Generates scores nobody reads. Coaching tab empty.
**Minimum viable activation:** Saved call library with curated examples. Managers review AI scores weekly with at least one coaching note per rep. Trackers for methodology terms, competitor names, objections. Connect scoring to knowledge layer.

### Tool Category 2: Revenue Signal / CRM Enrichment (Momentum, Clari)
**Should:** Extract intelligence from calls, enrich CRM, route signals in real time, trigger playbook guidance at stage changes.
**Usually does:** Enriches CRM. Generates signals nobody routes. Playbook content fields empty.
**Highest-ROI fix:** Every deal stage change = opportunity to surface battle card, discovery questions, next-step framework. Infrastructure exists. Content is usually empty.
**Loss reason blind spot:** Declining win rates almost always have loss reason capture off.

### Tool Category 3: Practice & Simulation (Exec AI)
**Should:** Rehearsal space. Surface skill gaps. Trigger practice when scoring reveals weakness.
**Usually does:** Runs during onboarding only. After onboarding, zero ongoing practice.
**Methodology alignment gap:** If call scoring measures SPIN, practice needs SPIN scenarios.

### Tool Category 4: Knowledge Base (Guru, Notion, Confluence)
**Should:** Single source of truth. Updated continuously. Linked from rest of stack.
**Usually does:** Content from launch, never updated. Placeholder text visible. Methodology scattered across 3-5 cards.
**Governance rules:**
- Every card has owner + verification date
- Methodology in ONE canonical card
- Live placeholder text = governance failure (audit quarterly)
- Battle cards update every time competitor changes anything
- Top call examples hyperlinked from methodology cards

### Tool Category 5: Execution (Salesloft, Outreach, Apollo)
**Should:** Deliver right sequence to right prospect. Curated library of proven plays.
**Usually does:** Thousands of cadences, no archiving, no naming convention. Top cadences invisible.
**Minimum viable governance:**
- Naming convention: Vertical - Persona - Sequence Type - Version Date
- Archive every cadence unused in 90 days
- 3-5 approved cadences per vertical
- Surface top cadences by contact-to-meeting rate, not raw volume
- Map CRM deal stages to closing funnel

**Red flags:** Personal names in team cadences. Version drift ("- Copy", "- Copy2"). Test entries still active. No archiving. No distinction between what works and what's just high-volume.

**Email performance reality:** High volume + low reply = messaging problem, not volume problem. 1-2% reply on high-volume templates means messaging is broken, volume is masking it.

## 19. RevOps Data Principles

**Data your business must answer at any time:**
1. Current pipeline coverage ratio? (3x min, 4x healthy)
2. Win rate by stage, vertical, rep?
3. Where are deals stalling? (Longest-average-age stage?)
4. Average sales cycle length?
5. Top 3 loss reasons this quarter?
6. Contact-to-meeting conversion by sequence?
7. Meeting-to-opportunity conversion?
8. Average deal size trend month-over-month?

**If you can't answer these, your CRM is a rolodex, not a revenue system.**

**Volume trap:** Big increase in opps created + drop in win rate = quality/execution problem hidden by volume. More activity isn't the answer. Better execution is.

## 20. Automation Principles

**Core:** Build output pipes, not just intake pipes.

**High-value automations in priority order:**
1. **Signal-to-action pipeline:** Trigger fires > CRM task + Slack alert + draft outreach. Respond <48 hrs.
2. **Pre-call brief generation:** Meeting booked > auto-enrich > structured brief to rep before call
3. **Daily pipeline digest:** Morning email -- who to call today, stale deals, missing next steps, approaching 50-day cliff
4. **Loss reason automation:** Every Closed Lost > automatic structured capture. Never manual.
5. **Post-call automation:** After recorded call > extract next steps, update CRM, notify stakeholders
6. **Bounce processing:** Email bounces > mark record, pause sequence, enrich, create task if no alternative

**Rule:** Webhooks > polling. Event-driven > scheduled. Acknowledge events immediately, do real work in background.

## 22. How to Apply This at Surge Clients

When building outreach systems, sales processes, or enablement programs for home service clients (HVAC, plumbing, roofing, restoration, etc.):

**Before recommending anything, ask:**
- Their collection rate and what's stuck where (like Rehab's $1.3M at 5.0)
- Who makes the buying decision in their customer's world (homeowner, property manager, insurance adjuster)
- Where their sales process breaks (lead response time, estimate follow-up, closing conversation, referral ask)
- What their current enablement looks like (training, playbooks, scripts -- usually none exist)

**Apply the flywheel to field service businesses:**
- **Reality Capture:** Call recordings from receptionist/dispatcher, tech ride-alongs, estimate presentations
- **Intelligence:** Which calls convert? Which techs close highest tickets? Which lead sources win?
- **Practice:** Role-play the in-home estimate conversation. Most techs have never practiced.
- **Knowledge:** One-pager on how to handle "I'll get other quotes." Scripts for upsells. Objection responses.
- **Execution:** Follow-up cadence for estimates that didn't close same-day (most contractors never follow up past 24 hrs)

**Home service SPIN examples:**
- Situation: "How many estimates does your team do per week?"
- Problem: "What percentage close same-day? What happens to the ones that don't?"
- Implication: "If you're losing 40% of estimates after day 1 and average job is $8K, what does that add up to per month?"
- Need-Payoff: "If you recovered half of those with a systematic follow-up, what would that mean for next quarter?"

**Home service trigger events to hunt:**
- New homeowner (recent address change -- high-intent for every trade)
- Recent home purchase in service area
- Storm damage alerts (roofers, restoration)
- Google Reviews complaining about competitor response time
- Competitor negative reviews on Nextdoor
- New subdivisions under construction
