## GOOGLE LOCAL SERVICE ADS (LSAs)

### How It Works
- LSAs appear above ALL Google Ads and organic results
- Pay-per-lead model (not pay-per-click) — $20–$150+ per lead depending on trade/market
- Google Verified badge: background check, license verification, insurance, business registration
- Google backs verified businesses with up to $2,000 guarantee to customers

### LSA Ranking Factors (in order)
1. **Review score** — single most important factor; maintain 4.8+ stars
2. **Review quantity and recency** — new reviews consistently over time
3. **Response rate** — answer calls fast; Google tracks response time
4. **Profile completeness** — all categories, services, photos, hours
5. **Booking rate** — mark leads as booked; aim for 80%+
6. **Service area targeting** — tighter geo focus outperforms broad
7. **GBP integration** — deeply linked, sync both accounts

### LSA Optimization Checklist
- Use **Maximize Leads** bidding (not Max Per Lead)
- Keep service area focused — start tight, expand as budget grows
- Enable all lead types: calls, messages, direct booking
- Dispute invalid leads promptly (wrong number, out of area, duplicates)
- Mark every lead as booked or not booked — trains the algorithm
- Add seasonal budget increases proactively
- Separate LSA accounts for multiple locations
- Connect GBP and LSA accounts to sync reviews

### 2025 Policy Note
As of April 2025, Google updated TOS to claim ownership of advertiser content in LSAs, including the right to record and analyze phone calls. Inform clients of this.

## GOOGLE BUSINESS PROFILE (GBP) — HIGHEST-ROI FREE ASSET

### Why GBP Is Critical
- 78% of local searches result in offline purchases within 24 hours
- 68% of local consumers contact business directly from GBP (no website visit)
- Map Pack generates 2–5x more clicks than organic results for local service queries
- GBP is now the primary data source Google's AI uses to answer local queries

### GBP Complete Optimization Checklist

**Profile Completeness:**
- Business name matches legal name exactly (NO keyword stuffing — Google penalizes)
- Primary category = highest-revenue service ("Plumber," "HVAC Contractor," "Roofing Contractor")
- All relevant secondary categories added
- Full 750-character business description with natural keyword integration
- Complete service area (all cities and zip codes)
- Complete hours including holiday hours
- Phone number matches website and all directories EXACTLY
- All services listed with descriptions and pricing where possible
- All business attributes filled (24/7, emergency services, veteran-led, women-led, etc.)

**Photos (Critical — Often Overlooked):**
- Minimum 10+ high-quality photos at launch; add 1–2 per month
- Team photos (humanizes the business)
- Branded trucks/vehicles
- Before/after project photos
- Work-in-progress shots
- Certifications and license plaques
- With photos: 45% more direction requests, 31% more website clicks

**Posts:**
- Post minimum 1–2 times per week (freshness is now an AI ranking signal)
- Include CTA in every post (Call Now, Book Online, Get Quote)
- Include photos in every post
- Seasonal content: furnace tune-ups in fall, AC checks in spring, storm damage for roofers
- **2025 alert:** No posts or photos in 30+ days = significant ranking drop

**Q&A / Ask Maps (2026):**
- Pre-populate Q&A with common questions and detailed answers
- Google replacing Q&A with "Ask Maps" powered by Gemini AI — consistency across GBP, website, and reviews is critical
- Seed with: "Do you offer emergency service?" "Are you licensed?" "What areas do you serve?" "Do you offer financing?"

## LOCAL SEO

### Citation Building Strategy

**Tier 1 — Core (Build First):**
Google Business Profile, Bing Places, Apple Maps, Yelp, BBB, Yellow Pages, Facebook Business, Foursquare, Nextdoor

**Tier 2 — Home Service Specific:**

**Data Aggregators (Submit Here First):**
Data Axle, Neustar Localeze, Foursquare, Factual — these distribute to hundreds of directories automatically

**NAP Consistency Rules:**
- Every listing must use IDENTICAL name, address, phone formatting
- "Ave" vs "Avenue" — all must match exactly
- Tools: BrightLocal, Moz Local, Whitespark for auditing
- Target: 40+ authoritative citations; top performers have 80–100+

### On-Page Local SEO

**Service Area Pages:**
- One unique page per major city/service area
- URL: `/plumber-dallas-tx/`, `/hvac-repair-fort-worth/`
- Must have unique content per page (NOT copy-paste with city name swapped)
- Include: local landmarks, neighborhoods, local reviews, embedded Google Map
- 500–800+ words per page; 1,000+ for competitive markets

**Homepage Optimization:**
- Primary keyword in H1 ("Austin's Trusted Plumbing Company")
- Phone number in header, clickable on mobile
- Service area mentioned in first 100 words
- Trust signals above the fold

**Schema Markup (JSON-LD format):**
- LocalBusiness schema — name, address, phone, hours, geo, service area, aggregate rating
- Service schema — individual pages per service type
- FAQPage schema — appears in AI Overviews
- HowTo schema — educational content
- AggregateRating schema — pulls star ratings into search results
- Validate with Google's Rich Results Test; check Search Console monthly

### Link Building for Home Services

**High-value links:**
- Local news coverage (sponsor youth sports, community events)
- Local Chamber of Commerce member listing (high DA, trusted by Google)
- Industry associations (PHCC, NRCA, NALP, NECA)
- Supplier/manufacturer "find a pro" pages
- Home builder and real estate agent referral pages
- Neighborhood association websites
- Local "best of" lists and awards

**Avoid:** Low-quality directory farms, paid link schemes, private blog networks

### Rank Tracking Tools
- **Local Falcon** — grid-based geo ranking visualization (best for client reports)
- **BrightLocal** — best all-in-one for local SEO agencies
- **Whitespark** — citation finder, rank tracker
- **Semrush** — broader SEO with local features

## AI SEARCH OPTIMIZATION (AEO / GEO) — THE FRONTIER

### The Current Landscape (2025–2026)
- AI referral traffic: 1.13 billion visits in June 2025 — **357% year-over-year growth**
- ChatGPT drives 87.4% of all AI referral traffic; has overtaken Wikipedia in monthly visits
- Google AI Overviews appear in 50–60% of U.S. searches
- AI Overview traffic converts at 14.2% vs. traditional organic's 2.8% — **5x quality premium**
- Perplexity: 6–10x higher CTR than ChatGPT with 20–30% conversion rates on high-intent queries

### How to Get Featured in ChatGPT
ChatGPT sources local data from:
1. **Bing index** — ChatGPT uses Bing for browsing; Bing SEO matters
2. **Yelp** — cited heavily by ChatGPT
3. **High-authority directories** — BBB, Angi, Houzz
4. **Your own website** — if ranking in Bing

**Action steps:**
- Claim and fully optimize **Bing Places for Business** (redesigned Oct 2025, at bing.com/forbusiness)
- Maintain strong Yelp presence — ChatGPT cites Yelp heavily
- Build content answering "best [trade] in [city]" queries
- Get cited in local media indexed by Bing
- Use structured data to help crawlers understand your business entity
- Microsoft Copilot + ChatGPT both pull from Bing (Microsoft owns ~49% of OpenAI)

### How to Get Featured in Claude / Perplexity / Other AI
- Build presence on authoritative platforms (Yelp, BBB, Angi, HomeAdvisor, local news)
- Maintain high review ratings across ALL platforms
- Well-structured website clearly stating service area, services, credentials
- Get mentioned in local press and industry publications
- Ensure NAP consistency so AI can confidently attribute your entity

### Google AI Overviews Optimization
- Lead every major content section with a "direct answer capsule" of 40–60 words
- Use clear H1/H2/H3 heading hierarchy
- FAQ sections with FAQPage schema markup
- Cite statistics with source links (AI prefers content that cites data)
- Keep content fresh — update dates matter
- Write for the specific question, not just the topic
- Listicles and structured content account for 32% of all AI citations

### Bing / Copilot Optimization (NEW — 2025)
- **Bing AI Performance in Webmaster Tools** — new tool showing how often your content is cited in AI answers
- Enable **IndexNow** — notifies Bing/Google instantly when content is updated
- Microsoft ecosystem now reaches 1.4 billion users across Bing, Edge, Outlook, LinkedIn
- Bing Places: less saturated than Google — high opportunity for local businesses
- Fill out every detail in Bing Places including amenities (e.g., hasWiFi) — Copilot checks these for AI answers

### Entity Optimization (Critical for All AI Search)
AI search relies on **entity understanding** — recognizing your business as a distinct, trustworthy entity.

**Entity signals:**
- Consistent NAP across ALL platforms (the more sources agree, the stronger the entity)
- Google Knowledge Panel (claim and optimize if available)
- Mentions in authoritative publications
- Active, consistent social media profiles
- Schema markup referencing recognized entities
- Identical business name on website, GBP, Yelp, BBB, Facebook — NO abbreviations or variations

### E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness)

**Experience signals:**
- "About Us" page with owner/technicians (real photos)
- First-hand content ("After 15 years fixing furnaces in Colorado winters...")
- Before/after photos and case studies
- Video of work being performed

**Expertise signals:**
- Trade certifications displayed prominently (NATE, EPA 608, licensed electrician)
- Author bios with credentials on blog posts
- Technical content showing real knowledge (not generic AI filler)
- Nuanced trade-specific FAQs

**Authoritativeness signals:**
- Backlinks from local news, trade publications, supplier websites
- Consistent presence across industry directories
- Local media coverage

**Trustworthiness signals:**
- Consistent NAP across web
- Google reviews (quantity, recency, rating)
- SSL/HTTPS
- Privacy policy and Terms of Service
- Physical address on website
- BBB accreditation
- Response to all reviews on all platforms

## GOOGLE ADS / PPC

### Campaign Structure
1. **LSA Campaign** (managed in LSA dashboard) — highest priority
2. **Branded Search** — protect brand from competitors
3. **Service-Specific Search** — separate per service line
4. **Emergency/Urgent Campaign** — high-intent emergency keywords, higher bids
5. **Competitor Campaign** (optional)
6. **Remarketing Display** — follow website visitors

### Emergency vs. Planned Keywords

**Emergency (highest priority — convert 3–5x higher):**
"emergency plumber near me," "AC repair same day," "24 hour electrician," "furnace repair tonight," "burst pipe emergency"
→ Allocate 30–40% of budget to emergency terms

**Planned service keywords:** "plumber [city]," "water heater replacement," "roof inspection near me"

**Negative keywords (add immediately):**
DIY, jobs, careers, training, free, how to, YouTube, Reddit, school, certification, parts, supply, wholesale, warranty claim

### 2025 Ad Benchmarks
- Average CTR: 6.37%
- Average CPC: $7.85 (electricians $12.18, painters $13.74, roofers $10.70)
- Target CPL: $50–$150 for most services
- Target ROAS: 3:1 minimum

### Smart Bidding Strategy
- First 30–60 days: **Maximize Conversions** (build data)
- After 30+ conversions/month/campaign: switch to **Target CPA**
- Set Target CPA at 150% of desired CPL initially, then tighten

### Required Ad Extensions
Call, Location, Sitelinks, Callouts ("Licensed & Insured," "Free Estimates," "24/7 Emergency"), Structured Snippets, Lead Form

### Budget Planning
- Small market (<100K population): $800–$2,000/month
- Mid-size market: $2,000–$6,000/month
- Major metro: $5,000–$20,000+/month

## REPUTATION MANAGEMENT

### Why Reviews Are Everything
- 88% of consumers read Google reviews before contacting a local business
- Businesses below 4.0 stars receive 70% fewer inquiries than 4.5+ businesses
- One-star increase in Yelp = 5–9% revenue increase
- LSA rankings primarily determined by review score and recency
- 88% of consumers prefer businesses that respond to ALL reviews

### Review Platform Priority
1. **Google** — 73% of all online reviews; 81% of consumers check Google; directly impacts LSA and Map Pack
2. **Yelp** — 178M monthly visitors; critical for ChatGPT/AI citations
3. **Angi/HomeAdvisor** — trade-specific; integrates with lead marketplace
4. **Facebook** — third most-used; important for social proof
5. **BBB** — trust signal for AI search entity authority

### Review Generation System
1. **Trigger:** Tech completes job, customer expresses satisfaction
2. **Verbal ask:** "Would you mind leaving us a quick Google review? It helps our small business."
3. **Immediate SMS:** Within 2 hours of job completion with direct Google review link
4. **Email follow-up:** If no review in 48 hours
5. **Second SMS:** 5 days later (maximum one additional follow-up)

**Automation tools:** Podium, Birdeye, NiceJob, GoHighLevel, ServiceTitan/Housecall Pro/Jobber (built-in)

**Target:** Minimum 1–2 new Google reviews per week per location; respond to 100% of reviews

### Responding to Negative Reviews
Framework:
1. Acknowledge the issue without admitting fault
2. Apologize for the experience
3. Take it offline: "Please call our customer service manager at [phone]"
4. Keep response under 100 words
5. NEVER argue or be defensive

Template: "Thank you for sharing your experience, [Name]. We're sorry to hear this didn't meet your expectations. We take all feedback seriously and would like to make this right. Please reach out at [phone] so we can address this personally."

## Code & Copy Voice

### Writing Style
- **Active voice, specific language** — not "manage clients" but "track Angi leads from first call to booked job"
- **Use real numbers** — not "increase revenue" but "add $174K/yr in recurring revenue"
- **Trade jargon where appropriate** — LSA, GBP, booking rate, CPL, Map Pack. Clients speak this language
- **Error messages must be real** — never "An error occurred." Say "Couldn't load client data — check your connection and try again."
- **Button copy = outcome** — not "Submit" but "Get My Blueprint" / not "Save" but "Save Changes"

### Code Style
- **Clarity over cleverness** — readable in 6 months by someone who didn't write it
- **Name things after outcomes** — `BookedLeadsChart` not `DataViz3`, `ReviewRequestModal` not `FormModal`
- **Comment the WHY, not the WHAT** — `// Mark lead as booked to train LSA algorithm` not `// sets value to true`
- **Prefer explicit state** — `isSubmitting`, `hasError`, `showSnapshot` over vague booleans
- **Realistic seed data** — use real-sounding business names, actual service types, plausible metrics

### UI & Design Voice
- Animations: **subtle and purposeful** — entrance fades, hover lifts. Never decorative spinning or bouncing
- Trust signals above the fold — review stars, years in business, license info visible without scrolling
- Forms: **inline validation**, no surprise errors after submit
- Mobile first — every CTA clickable with a thumb, phone numbers always `tel:` links
- **Feel like a real startup**, not a design system showcase — intentional imperfections welcome

### Tone
- Direct and confident — this agency has 30+ years of home service expertise baked in
- Avoid hedging language ("might," "could potentially," "it's possible that")
- Specific > generic, always. Replace every placeholder with real content before delivering
